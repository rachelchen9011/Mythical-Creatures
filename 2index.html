Throughout the world, humans have always feared and adored mythical creatures. These interesting creatures are supernatural animals, beings, or hybrids that don't exist in real life.

Dragons

  • can have four legs at most and sometimes even none.
  • have scales, claws, and possibly spikes.
  • have long tails and necks.
  • have huge mouths and sharp carnivorous teeth.
  • can fly.

Unicorns

  • are small but strong.
  • are powered by the sun.
  • usually stay in herds.
  • have been around for a long, long time.
  • are mulicolored.

Mermaids

  • are half human, half fish.
  • sometimes have superpowers like telepathy and hypnosis.
  • were first seen by Christopher Columbus.
  • fear fire.
  • live a few centuries.

Fairies

  • like shiny rocks.
  • vulnerable to iron and silver.
  • can have specific jobs, like the Tooth Fairy.
  • are many different sizes.
  • usually don't have wings. They use their magic to help them fly.

Griffins

  • are half eagle half lion.
  • are believed to have pulled the chariot of the god Zeus and Apollo.
  • are protectors of evil.
  • live up to 12-15 years.
  • guard treasure.

Pegasus

  • is immortal.
  • is a winged horse.
  • can produce water out of his hooves.
  • is the favourite of Zeus.
  • is pure white.

Basilisks

  • can turn anyone to stone if they look into its eyes.
  • defeatable when they have a mirror reflected at them.
  • come from hatching a chicken egg underneath a toad.
  • are insectivores, meaning they mainly eat insects.
  • can shape-shift.

Sea Serpents

  • were thouht to be gigantic snakes.
  • mainly eat fish and fish eggs.
  • are normally a dark navy blue.
  • have a power of enhanced agility.
  • can grow to be about 100 feet.
